FAQ

Short answers to predictable objections. This project is not a vibe. It is a rule.

Is this just “substantive due process” in disguise?

No. The proposal does not rewrite the Fourteenth Amendment or require any particular test. It simply restates the original Ninth Amendment rule: enumeration is not exhaustion, and unenumerated status is not a reason to deny a right.

Does this create unlimited rights with no constraint?

No. It denies only a categorical move: “not enumerated” or “not historically recognized” as a sufficient reason, by itself, to reject a claim. Courts still evaluate scope, limits, and conflicts under ordinary constitutional analysis.

Why include a list of examples in Section 2?

Because the doctrine has drifted. The list is illustrative, not exhaustive, and it signals the kinds of interests the Ninth Amendment was always meant to protect without turning the Amendment into a menu.

Does this diminish the Fourteenth Amendment?

No. Section 4 is an explicit savings clause: nothing here reduces equal protection or due process protections.

Is this self-executing?

Yes. Section 5 says so explicitly and rejects “no implementing statute” as an excuse to avoid adjudication.

Can Congress still legislate in this space?

Yes. Section 6 preserves congressional power to enforce, but makes that power supplementary rather than a prerequisite for judicial enforcement.
